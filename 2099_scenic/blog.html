This is a first in a while since I went outside to eat some of my favorite foods (Ramen). For this blog I'm going to show y'all my Top 2 best ramen spots in manila and a couple of snacks along the way.

Lunch

The first ramen place I went to was Ramen Nagi. Before quarantine I would always eat at this place weekly. This is an all time classic for me.

I ordered the Butao King, which is their original flavor, and my favorite (yes I'm a basic). It is made up of: Chashu (shoulder), Green Onions, Fire Sauce, Kikurage, which is a type of mushroom, hard noodles, and their rich broth. Keep in mind that these ingredients I listed are completely customizable. For example, you can change the Green Onions to be Green Cabbages, noodles can either be extra hard, normal, soft, or extra soft, the level of spiciness, and so much more. This is what I love about Ramen Nagi, the customization of your preferred ramen, and why it is my go to ramen place.






Snack

Before I head to my next ramen shop, I'd like to slow down and eat some light snacks. I went to de original Jamaican Pattie Shop. They sell these beef and tuna patties in a soft shell case sort of like the shell for empanadas. I went for their Cheesy Beef which is their original flavor, but with some much needed cheese. What I love most about their patties is the shell. It has the right amount of crunch that compliments the savory filling inside very well.






Dessert/Drink

For my dessert/drink, I went to a healthier option which is Jamba Juice (believe me, I'm surpised too). I ordered their Protein Berry Workout with Whey Protein. It is made up of Strawberries, Banana, Soy Milk, and Whey Protein. This was also a go to drink for me back when everything was normal. I would order this frequently after going to the gym.






Dinner

The Last place I went to for my food trip was Mendokoro Ramenba. If you can't already tell, I love Ramen. If there is a chance to eat ramen twice in one day, I would do it in a heartbeat.

Anyways, this place is much more hyped than Ramen Nagi mainly because of the hour long queue one has to endure before they get into the ramen bar. But trust me, it is always worth the wait. I ordered their Shoyu ramen which is a soy based broth. This is slightly more expensive than the first place I went to, but every drop of penny is worth it. The differences of this ramen place to the first one is that their ramen is significantly more richer. Also, their pork is charred, compared to the other one which is just broiled. As much as I loved this ramen, I can only eat here twice or thrice a month because if I ate here regularly I'm pretty sure I would die in a month.

Conclusion

It is always nice to go back and eat on some of your favorite places pre-quarantine especially since I'm at home 24/7 since March 2020. In the future, once the pandemic is over, or at least the number of people who are covid positive dramatically decrease, I would defenitely do this again, and review more Ramen places with friends, but only when my wallet is full again.
